Do you offer online therapy?

Do you provide treatment for adults as well?

Yes! We offer ADHD therapy for adults to improve focus, time management, and emotional regulation. Our therapists use CBT and executive function coaching to help with productivity, relationships, and daily challenges. Sessions are available online and in-person.

Do you provide treatment for children & teens?

Yes! We offer ADHD therapy for children and teens to improve focus, behaviour, and emotional regulation. Our therapists use CBT, behavioural therapy, and parent coaching to help kids succeed at home, in school, and socially. Sessions are available online and in-person.

How do I know if my child may have ADHD?

Common signs include trouble following instructions, difficulty sitting still, frequent forgetfulness, acting without thinking, and strong emotional reactions.

If these challenges are starting to affect schoolwork, friendships, or family life, it's a good idea to seek a professional assessment.Our team offers gentle, family-centered evaluations and therapy to help you get clear answers and supportive next steps.

Is therapy covered by insurance?

ADHD assessments and follow-up therapy are covered by most extended health benefit plans provided by work-place benefit plans or benefit plans which you pay into monthly.

Me or my child already have a diagnosis. Can you still help?

We offer therapy to help manage ADHD. Our therapists can give you or your child tools and strategies to improve focus and handle challenges in school, work, and daily life.

Is ADHD different in girls vs boys?

Yes, ADHD can look different in girls. Boys are often more hyperactive and impulsive, while girls are more likely to struggle quietly with inattention, daydreaming, and emotional regulation. As a result, ADHD in girls can be harder to spot and sometimes goes undiagnosed longer.

Is ADHD different in women vs men?

Yes, ADHD can present differently in women and men. Women are more likely to experience inattentive symptoms, such as difficulty focusing, disorganization, and overwhelm, while men often show more hyperactive and impulsive traits. Because ADHD in women is less obvious, it is often underdiagnosed or misdiagnosed.

Our therapists provide tailored treatment to help individuals manage ADHD based on their unique experiences and challenges.

Is therapy confidential?

Therapy is confidential. However, with children, therapists ensure safety and well-being first. We'll discuss general progress with parents and share specific details only with the child's consent or if there's a safety concern.

ADHD Treatment Sessions

Why would my child need ADHD Treatment?

If ADHD symptoms like trouble focusing, impulsivity, or emotional outbursts are affecting your child's success at home or school, targeted therapy can make a real difference. Our sessions help kids build focus, manage emotions, and develop daily skills. We also coach parents, giving you simple tools to support your child at home and create calmer routines.

5 Ways ADHD Treatment Can Help
  • Build Focus and Attention: Practical strategies to help kids stay on task at school and at home.
  • Manage Emotions: Tools for coping with frustration, impulsivity, and overwhelm.
  • Strengthen Social Skills: Support for making friends, handling peer challenges, and building confidence.
  • Improve Organization and Routines: Skills for completing homework, staying organized, and following daily routines.
  • Create a Calmer Home Life: Parent coaching to help manage behaviors and strengthen family communication.
How does ADHD Treatment work?

We use evidence-based approaches like CBT and behavior therapy, always tailored to your child's needs. Sessions are practical, interactive, and focused on building skills that last beyond therapy. Our goal is to help your child thrive at school, at home, and in their relationships. We offer both in-person and online options to fit your family's needs.

ADHD Assessments

Why would my child need an ADHD Assessment?

If your child struggles with focus, organization, impulsivity, or emotional regulation at home or school, an ADHD assessment can help you get clear answers. Our assessments identify strengths and challenges, helping you plan the right support for success in everyday life.

5 Benefits of a Professional ADHD Assessment
  • Clear Answers: Evidence-based testing to understand if ADHD is affecting your child's development.
  • Personalized Support Plan: Practical next steps for home, school, and therapy, tailored to your child's needs.
  • School Accommodations: Detailed reports that can support IEPs, extra help, or modifications at school.
  • Highlight Strengths: We identify not just challenges, but also your child's natural abilities.
  • Better Family Understanding: Insights that help parents guide and support daily routines and behavior.
How does an ADHD Assessment work?

Our process includes a detailed clinical interview with parents, standardized behavior questionnaires, and structured observations of your child. We assess attention, behavior, and executive functioning across different situations to build a full picture of your child's strengths and needs. You'll receive a comprehensive report, diagnosis (if applicable), and an easy-to-follow action plan. Assessments are available in-person or online.
