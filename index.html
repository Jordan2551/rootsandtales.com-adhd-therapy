What is your ADHD assessment process?

1. Parent Intake Session (Approximately 1 hour): An initial meeting to explore your child's developmental history, current challenges, and strengths. This helps guide the assessment plan.

2. Standardized Questionnaires (Completed over several days): Parents, teachers, and (depending on age) the child complete questionnaires about attention, impulsivity, and behaviour at home and school. This offers a well-rounded understanding of your child's needs.

3. In-Person Testing (Approximately 3 hours): Your child completes interactive tasks with a psychologist and psychometrist to assess attention, working memory, and executive functioning. These sessions identify cognitive patterns related to focus and self-regulation.

4. Feedback Session (About 1 hour total): A follow-up meeting to review results, discuss findings, and plan next steps. When appropriate, a shorter, age-appropriate feedback session may be held with your child.

5. Final Report (Delivered within 1‚Äì2 weeks): A comprehensive written report summarizing results, diagnostic impressions, and practical recommendations to support your child at home, school, and in daily life.

Is ADHD different in girls vs boys?

Yes, ADHD can look different in girls. Boys are often more hyperactive and impulsive, while girls are more likely to struggle quietly with inattention, daydreaming, and emotional regulation. As a result, ADHD in girls can be harder to spot and sometimes goes undiagnosed longer.

Is ADHD different in women vs men?

Yes, ADHD can present differently in women and men. Women are more likely to experience inattentive symptoms, such as difficulty focusing, disorganization, and overwhelm, while men often show more hyperactive and impulsive traits. Because ADHD in women is less obvious, it is often underdiagnosed or misdiagnosed.

Our therapists provide tailored treatment to help individuals manage ADHD based on their unique experiences and challenges.

Is therapy confidential?

Therapy is confidential. However, with children, therapists ensure safety and well-being first. We'll discuss general progress with parents and share specific details only with the child's consent or if there's a safety concern.

Neurofeedback Training

What is Neurofeedback?

Neurofeedback is a drug-free, brain-based training method that helps improve self-regulation and cognitive performance. We create a detailed map of your child's brain activity, generating a visual representation of how their brain functions. Using this real-time feedback, your child's brain learns to function more efficiently, leading to better focus, emotional control, and reduced impulsivity.

How Neurofeedback Helps with ADHD

Neurofeedback training can lead to improvements in:

  • Better Attention and Focus: Train your brain to stay on task longer and more effectively.
  • Improved Emotional Control: Reduce emotional outbursts and build calmer responses to stress.
  • Reduced Impulsivity: Strengthen self-control and think before acting.
  • More Efficient Brain Functioning: Optimize how your brain processes information and responds to challenges.
A Great Complement or Alternative to Medication

Many families choose neurofeedback as a non-invasive alternative to medication, or as a complement to existing treatment. It's safe, backed by research, and can lead to lasting improvements in attention and self-regulation.

Is Neurofeedback Right for Your Child?

Neurofeedback is suitable for children, teens, and adults with ADHD. Our team will assess whether it's a good fit for your child during your initial consultation and create a personalized training plan if appropriate.

Parent Training for ADHD

Why Parent Training Matters

Empowering parents is one of the strongest predictors of long-term success for children with ADHD. When parents understand ADHD and learn effective strategies, the entire family benefits by creating a calmer, more supportive home environment.

What You'll Learn

Our 50-minute parent training sessions help you develop practical skills to:

  • Support Routines and Structure at Home: Build consistent, predictable daily schedules that reduce stress.
  • Improve Communication with Your Child: Learn how to connect, listen, and respond in ways that build trust and cooperation.
  • Reduce Conflict and Frustration: De-escalate challenging moments and prevent power struggles before they start.
  • Reinforce Positive Behaviors: Use positive reinforcement and encouragement to shape better habits.
  • Help with Homework and Organization: Strategies to make schoolwork less overwhelming for both you and your child.
  • Understand How ADHD Affects Emotions and Daily Life: Gain insight into your child's brain and behavior, so you can respond with empathy and confidence.
Parents Gain Confidence, Kids Feel Supported

When you feel equipped and confident, your child feels more understood and supported. Our parent training sessions give you the tools to create lasting positive change at home, reducing stress and strengthening your relationship with your child.
